# E156-precision-sweep (N/A (characterisation, not benchmark))

At what input-precision level does aggregate-data reproduction of fixed-effects 2-trial pools become tolerance-limited by rounding alone, independent of implementation correctness? Ten thousand Monte Carlo 2-trial pools: true log-HR uniform over [log 0.6, log 1.3], per-trial SE uniform [0.04, 0.08], xoshiro128** seed 20260415. Each pool rounded at dp in {1,2,3,4} decimal places on both HR and CI bounds, re-pooled via fixed-effects inverse-variance on log-HR, compared to the unrounded pool. Median |Δ| at dp=1/2/3/4: 0.0179/0.0016/0.0002/0.0000; 95th %ile: 0.0469/0.0044/0.0004/0.0000; at dp=2, 2.4% of pools exceed δ=0.005. Floor scales monotonically with 10⁻ᵈᵖ across all four levels; both median and 95th percentile fall by approximately a decade per dp increment. Benchmark-reproduction tolerance must exceed the precision floor at the input dp; δ=0.005 at dp=2 lies above the 0.0044 p95 yet below max 0.0084, explaining the prior SGLT2i FAIL as a tail outcome, not a defect. Scope: k=2, fixed-effects, unimodal true-HR distribution; random-effects, k>2, and IPD precision floors remain open questions.
